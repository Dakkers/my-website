The Collatz Conjecture

"Mathematics is not yet ripe enough for such problems." ~ Paul Erdos (allegedly)

This webpage is for an interactive doohickey for the Collatz conjecture, which, in my opinion, is one of the most interesting unsolved problems in mathematics (but perhaps I am just a boring person).

The Collatz conjecture (also known as the 3n+1 conjecture) states this: start with a positive integer n. If n is even, divide it by 2; if n is odd, multiply it by 3 and add 1. Repeat this process. n will eventually reach 1, and then go to 4, and then to 2, and then 1, etc. More formally speaking, the iterates of n will eventually converge to the 3-cycle {4, 2, 1}.
